About Me

Earth Scientist Passionate About Climate Solutions

I am a final-year BS-MS student at IISER Bhopal, specialising in CO₂ sequestration in basaltic formations. My thesis involves developing and validating a 1D reactive transport model using Python-PHREEQC to simulate CO₂ mineralisation, porewater chemistry evolution, and porosity-permeability coupling.

With hands-on experience in seismic data analysis, GIS, and machine learning, I bring an interdisciplinary approach to geoscience problems. I am actively applying for PhD positions across Europe, the UK, and Australia.

Research Interests

Areas I Am Passionate About

My research interests span the intersection of subsurface geoscience, climate solutions, and computational modelling — areas I look forward to exploring at the doctoral level.

01

Geomechanics & Subsurface Integrity

Understanding stress-state changes during fluid injection, their impact on fault stability and caprock integrity, and coupling geomechanical frameworks with reactive transport models for safe reservoir management.

GeomechanicsFault StabilityRock Mechanics
02

Reservoir Modelling & CO₂ Storage

Developing multiscale reservoir models to predict CO₂ trapping efficiency, mineralisation rates, and porewater geochemistry evolution in heterogeneous basalt and sedimentary formations.

Reservoir ModellingCO₂ StoragePHREEQC
03

Carbon Capture & Storage (CCS)

Optimising CO₂ injection strategies: single-burst, continuous, and WAG flooding to maximise mineral trapping while minimising leakage risk, with a focus on basalt-hosted storage in volcanic provinces.

CCSInjection StrategyMineral Trapping
04

Structural Geology & Tectonics

Investigating how fault networks, fracture systems, and stratigraphic architecture in volcanic and sedimentary provinces control fluid migration pathways, with applications to resource management and storage site selection.

Structural GeologyTectonicsFault Systems
05

Seismology & Geophysical Monitoring

Applying passive seismic monitoring and shear wave splitting to characterise subsurface anisotropy, track fluid-induced changes, and detect induced seismicity at injection and geothermal sites.

SeismologyShear Wave SplittingInduced Seismicity
06

Computational & Data-Driven Geoscience

Integrating Python-based reactive transport models, machine learning surrogates, and geospatial tools to accelerate geoscience workflows from seismic hazard assessment to geochemical simulation and field data analysis.

Computational GeoscienceML / AIPython Modelling
